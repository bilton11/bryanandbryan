Notes on Fee Categories

Infrequent Claimant
A person or company that files fewer than 10 claims per calendar year. This is the standard category for most individuals and small businesses.
Frequent Claimant
A person or company that files 10 or more claims per calendar year (e.g. landlords, utilities). Higher fees apply as a volume disincentive.
Certificate of Judgment
Issued after you obtain a judgment. Used to enforce the judgment (e.g. garnishment, seizure).
Writ of Delivery / Seizure and Sale
Court-ordered enforcement documents. A Writ of Seizure and Sale directs the sheriff to seize and sell the debtor's property to satisfy the judgment.

Source: Ontario Regulation 432/93 (Small Claims Court — Fees), as amended. View regulation on ontario.ca
